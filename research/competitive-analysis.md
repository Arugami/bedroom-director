### 2025 UI & Monetization Observations (from TAFT, FutureTools, Futurepedia, Toolify, Dang!, Insidr)
- **Command-center heroes:** All lead with giant search bars plus stacked CTAs, context toggles, and trust stats. We'll mirror the approachable entry point but add "Tested on set" metrics.
- **Paid placements everywhere:** Sponsored hero cards, "Matt's Picks" slots, right-rail promos, and newsletter mentions show how aggressively these teams sell visibility. We can monetize similarly but tie placements to verified benchmarks so credibility stays intact.
- **Workspace-lite UX:** Persistent side rails, mode toggles (Personal/Work), and "My Tools" docks keep discovery fluid. We'll extend this idea with comparison queues and saved workflows.
- **Community signals:** Upvotes, karma, bookmark counts, and "recently verified" labels give social proof even when the content is shallow. We'll capture real operator data instead of vanity numbers.
- **Depth gap:** None of the directories provide QA notes, footage examples, or structured comparisons. This is where we go far deeper than anyone else.

## üí° Key Insights

### What Works (from competitors):

1. **Freemium model** - All successful platforms use it
2. **Community-driven content** - User-generated prompts scale better
3. **Search + Filters** - Essential for discovery
4. **User profiles** - Recognition motivates contribution
5. **Mobile-first** - Most traffic is mobile

### What Doesn't Work:

1. **Ads only** - Insufficient revenue
2. **Marketplace only** - Limits community sharing
3. **Single model focus** - Users need cross-model comparison
4. **Technical jargon** - Scares away beginners
5. **No guidance** - Users struggle without help
