## ðŸŽ¨ Visual Identity

### **Core Aesthetic:**
**"Theme park at twilight"** - That magical moment when neon signs turn on against a purple dusk sky. Cinematic + nostalgic, sophisticated + emotional.

**Design Philosophy:**
- **Palm tree silhouettes** - ESSENTIAL brand DNA. Creates Hollywood/Florida/Miami noir aesthetic (Scarface, Drive, Miami Vice). Not decoration - these frame every scene and connect California dreams + Florida theme parks + 80s rebellion.
- **Practical neon lighting** - Real glowing effects, visible halos, colored uplighting
- **Twilight atmosphere** - Purple/blue gradient skies, dusk/golden hour mood
- **Multi-color storytelling** - Cool tones (purple/blue) + warm accents (yellow/orange/green)
- **Theatrical presentation** - Like entering a theme park attraction
- **Cinematic depth** - Film grain, atmospheric haze, layered lighting
- **NOT:** Cheesy VHS glitches, over-the-top effects, flat modern minimalism

**Inspiration:**
- Hollywood Tower Hotel (elegant neon signage at dusk)
- Universal Studios Orlando at 7pm (palm trees + neon just turned on, sky still glowing)
- Blockbuster Video exterior (confident chunky signage)
- Disney World entrance at twilight (magical atmosphere)
- Scarface, Drive, Miami Vice (80s Miami noir with palm tree silhouettes)

### **Color Palette:**

**Primary - Moody Cool:**
- **Director Black** - #000000 (pure black, deep backgrounds)
- **Director Black Soft** - #0a0a0a (subtle variation)
- **Bedroom Purple** - #7C3AED (muted cinematic purple)
- **Purple Bloom** - #8B5CF6 (subtle bloom accent)
- **Purple Soft** - #6366F1 (UI elements)

**Accent Neons - Warm Pops:**
- **Retro Teal** - #00CED1 (Hollywood Tower signage)
- **Sunset Orange** - #FF8C42 (golden hour warmth)
- **Neon Yellow** - #FCD34D (spotlight accent)
- **Action Red** - #EF4444 (CTA, energy)

**Atmospheric:**
- **Screen White** - #FFFFFF (pure white)
- **Twilight Sky** - Purple/blue gradients (not solid colors)

### **Signature Visual Elements:**
1. **Palm Trees** - Core brand identity. Always use as framing device (silhouettes against twilight). Represents Hollywood dreams (California), theme park magic (Florida), and rebellious creativity (80s Miami noir). Essential to every major design.
2. **Neon Typography** - Glowing wordmarks with visible halos and colored shadows
3. **Twilight Gradients** - Purple/blue dusk skies (never solid colors)
4. **Director's Chair** - Symbol of the bedroom director
5. **Practical Lighting Effects** - LED glows, colored uplighting, atmospheric haze

### **Typography:**
- **Headlines:** Bold sans-serif with neon text effects (Inter Bold, Outfit Bold)
  - Glowing outlines, visible halos, text shadows
  - Mix of chunky bold + elegant script for variety
- **Body:** Clean, readable sans-serif (Inter, System UI)
- **Neon Treatment:** Text should feel illuminated, not flat
  - Add glow effects, colored shadows, depth
  - Think: Lit-up marquee letters, not printed text

### **Logo (FINAL DECISION):**

**Wordmark Only - Clean, Bold, Timeless**

**Primary Logo:**
```
BEDROOM DIRECTOR
```
- Font: Inter Bold or Outfit Bold (700 weight)
- Tracking: -2% (tight but readable)
- Color: White on Director Black (#0F172A) or Black on white
- Style: All caps, minimal, cinematic

**Monogram (for small spaces):**
```
BD
```
- Same font, use for favicon, social icons, app icon

**With Tagline:**
```
BEDROOM DIRECTOR
From bedroom to big screen
```
- Tagline in lighter weight (400), 60% of logo size

**Philosophy:** The name IS the brand. No icons needed. Like A24, Apple, Supreme - let the work speak.
